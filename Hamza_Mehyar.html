I.   The leader I watched, the person I noticed

When I try to think of someone close to perfection — not the idea of it, but the actual practice of it — I think of you. Not because you have no flaws, but because the way you move through hard things is something I haven't seen anywhere else.

You carry your own life — your engagement, your family, everything that comes with being a full person — while holding this team together every single day. And you never make it look like a burden. I genuinely don't know how you do that.

You gave me confidence in skills I didn't know how to name yet. You asked my opinion on process decisions when I was still just an analyst — not because you had to. That's just what a real leader does, even unconsciously. Every piece of advice I carried into the next week thinking "oh, that makes more sense now" — that was yours. Those moments added up to more than you probably realize.

III.   A Note From Me

What I've always meant to say.

You always say that you fully separate personal life from work life — let me tell you that I partially disagree. I don't think a human being is capable of handling the same people 8.5 hours a day, 5 days a week, without developing some kind of attachment with them, or to that workplace.

I've seen you stay late 2–3 hours past your shift a lot of times — not to complete work, but to comfort one of us, or to answer our questions and concerns. You never complained about it.

الله لا يضيّعلك تعب — the efforts you mentally put into taking care of us is something I'll always appreciate. Every detail was noticed.

The hours we spent adjusting the KPIs and SMGs every few months, or process mapping a new project, or adding a new layer to our audits — that's truly what I like most about this department.

While most people don't see that — you're the sweetest person I've ever met, yet the roughest, and I don't know how you managed to mix both together. Maybe some details are not noticed by everyone, but I know that you genuinely care about us and the department. So maybe here comes our part to deliver that image to our subordinates. (I'm truly sorry).

When I think about it more, you're probably the only person that never hurt me, or planned to, or had hard feelings for me — within Quality at least — or at least I've never seen that from you.

I've seen you talk roughly with people when they deserved it. I cannot remember a single time you spoke to me that way — not once, even when I absolutely deserved it. I don't take that lightly.

Enmanuel and the team — because of Enmanuel, lol — keep saying I'm like your son. I never liked that. I've always seen you like my older brother — the one I'd resort to for advice, but not all kinds of advice, only when life feels like a bitch and you need a real one, from someone who actually cares.

Thank you, Hamza. I truly appreciate you — not only as a manager, but as a brother.

Eid is one of those events where we'll have a chance every now and then to share some beautiful words with each other. So I'll use that.
