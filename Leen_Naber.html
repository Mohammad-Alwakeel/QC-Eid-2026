Who You Are

There are people who are good at their job. And then there are people like you — who bring something to the room that no job description could ever capture.

Intelligence that cuts through noise. Judgment that rarely misses. A way of thinking that always, somehow, gravitates toward the solution rather than the problem. Easy to work with. Impossible to replace.

And underneath all of that — a heart that is genuinely, unguardedly good. The kind that cares about people before caring about outcomes. That's rarer than any professional skill you have. And you have a lot of those.

A Note From Me

Leen —

I want to say something I don't say enough, and that you probably don't hear enough: you deserve to rest.

Not as a reward. Not after you finish everything on the list. Just because you're a person, and rest is something you're allowed to have.

I've watched you carry more than your share for a long time — the workload, the late nights, the sleeping-sitting-up situation that should never happen to anyone, ever. You give everything to everything, and somewhere in all of that, you forget to give anything to yourself.

I hope this break gives you back something you've been missing. The gym. The sleep. The version of yourself that existed before the workload decided to become a personality trait. That person is still there. She just needs a little space to breathe.

And I want you to know — separate from all of it, separate from work and Quality and ATAs that you very understandably hate with your whole soul — that knowing you has been one of the genuinely good parts of these years. You are one of the rare people where the more time passes, the more clearly I see how good you actually are.

Not good at the job. Good as a person.

I wish you everything, Leen. The gym, the Acai, the Hungarian walking lifestyle, the Mexican salad from Salma whenever you want it, and a morning — many mornings — where you wake up and actually feel like yourself again.

You deserve every good thing. Simply because of how pure your heart is.

Something you once said. That we pinned. That I keep thinking about.
