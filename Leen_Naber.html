When I Was Your Analyst
01

How to Write Like an Auditor, Not a Spotter

I was writing comments that were too direct. Too personal. You sat with me and changed it — not just the wording, but the entire mindset behind the work.

"Always start with: Agent should always be advised to never... — not: Agent should never." Use they/them instead of she/he. It stays objective. It stays fair. And never point the finger — act like someone who gives the benefit of the doubt. "Agent probably had technical issues. Please make sure to coach them on follow-up calls."

I will never forget those. They taught me how to think about this work. Not what to write — how to think. Thank you, Leen.
